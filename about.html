About Me

Name: Alvin Tran
Occupation: 4A Software Engineering Student
School: University of Waterloo

I've been interested in software design and development since my elementary school days (roughly starting from when I was 10 years old), and much of this interest stems from my interests in video games at the time. My interest in video games eventually led me to become more intrigued with how they were made, eventually piquing my interest in video game development. This, in turn, sparked my interest in the programming aspect of video game design, which encouraged me to take several computer science classes in high school (including Advanced Placement Computer Science in senior year), further exposing me to such topics as algorithm design, object-oriented programming, and even webpage design. Eventually, I came to realize that my future lied in the world of software development, and as such, I decide to attend the University of Waterloo in Software Engineering, where I now stand as a 3A student.

I consider myself a very practical learner. As a result, I feel I learn best when I get as much hands-on experience with whatever it is I am learning, rather than simply reading or hearing about it. This has influenced my decision to choose the University of Waterloo as my school of choice over several others, as Waterloo's co-op program allows me to get as much hands-on experience with software development and design in the working world as I want. It is also the reason why I've chosen Software Engineering as my career choice, as software design and development is a field that requires much practical experience to truly excel at it.

Thus far, my favourite courses during my undergraduate career at the University of Waterloo have been "Algorithms and Data Structures," "Digital Computers," "Object Oriented Programming," "Foundations of Sequential Programs," "Operating Systems," and "User Interfaces." I am also currently taking a course in "Co-operative and Adaptive Algorithms," which I find to be the most intriguing of my current courses, at the moment.

If you would like to know more about my work experience and projects, please take a look at my resume, the projects page, my LinkedIn profile, or even just e-mail me at a2tran@uwaterloo.ca.
