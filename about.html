About Me

Name: Alvin Tran
Occupation: Software Developer at Hulu
School: University of Waterloo, Software Engineering 2014

My interest in software design and development can probably be traced back to elementary school days, stemming from my interests in video games at the time. My interest in video games eventually led me to become more intrigued with how they were made, eventually piquing my interest in video game development. This, in turn, sparked my interest in the programming aspect of video game design, which encouraged me to take several computer science classes in high school, exposing me to such topics as algorithm design, object-oriented programming, and web development. Eventually, I came to realize that my future lied in the world of software development, and as such, I decided to attend the University of Waterloo for Software Engineering. I have since graduated in 2014.

I mostly chose the University of Waterloo because of its renowned co-op program. I feel I learn best through hands-on experience, and Waterloo's co-op program allows me to get as much hands-on experience with software development and design in the working world as I want. It is also the reason why I've chosen Software Engineering as my career choice, as software design and development is a field that requires much practical experience to truly excel at it.

My favourite courses during my undergraduate career at the University of Waterloo have been "Algorithms and Data Structures," "Digital Computers," "Object Oriented Programming," "Foundations of Sequential Programs," "Operating Systems," "User Interfaces," "Co-operative and Adaptive Algorithms," and "Computer Security and Privacy."

If you would like to know more about my work experience and projects, please take a look at my resume, the projects page, my LinkedIn profile, or just e-mail me at althaitran@gmail.com.
